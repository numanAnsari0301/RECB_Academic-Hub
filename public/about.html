About the College

Rajkiya Engineering College (formerly Dr. Bhim Rao Ambedkar Engineering College of Information Technology, Bijnor) was started by the Government of Uttar Pradesh, Department of Technical Education under the Special Component Plan (SCP) — a plan of the Union Government of India for the promotion of Technical Education in the socially and economically weaker sections of society.


Admissions to the College began in the year 2010–11 with three branches: Civil Engineering, Electrical Engineering and Information Technology, with an intake of 60 students in each branch. The college is affiliated to Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Technical University (AKTU), Lucknow.

Vision

"To be recognized as a premier institution in the field of Engineering and Technology, to provide industry-ready technocrats with distinctive competence and ethical standards for the development and social welfare of society."

🎯

Mission

  • 1To develop innovative and competent engineers for the growth of industry and society.
  • 2To sustain an institutional environment in which innovative ideas, research and consultancy develop and from which the innovators of tomorrow emerge.
  • 3To address socio-technical problems faced by the state through the talent we nurture.
  • 4To serve the local community through the use of local resources.
  • 5To encourage dissemination and discovery of knowledge in an atmosphere of scientific freedom.
  • 6To actively involve in rural development by way of involvement in technology-based solutions for problems in rural areas.
